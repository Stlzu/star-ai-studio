### Post 6: How to Pick What to Automate in Your Business (Educational)

**Hook:**
Not every process should be automated. But the ones that should — can save you 40+ hours a month.

Here's the exact framework I use to identify high-ROI automation opportunities 👇

**Body:**

A question I get constantly: "What should I automate in my business?"

Here's my 4-part framework:

**1. Frequency check**
How often does this task happen?
- Daily or weekly = high priority
- Monthly or quarterly = medium priority
- Once a year = probably not worth it

**2. Time drain check**
How long does it take per occurrence?
- 30+ minutes = high priority
- 5–30 minutes = medium priority
- < 5 minutes = skip (unless it happens 50x/day)

**3. Rule clarity check**
Can you describe the process step-by-step?
- Yes, it's mechanical and repeatable = excellent automation candidate
- It requires subjective judgment every time = keep human in loop

**4. Error sensitivity check**
What's the cost of a mistake?
- A typo in an invoice costs your client money = high priority
- A wrong lead enrichment field causes a bad sales call = medium priority
- A slightly wrong blog headline = low priority

**The sweet spot?**
Tasks that score high on ALL four: frequent, time-consuming, rule-based, error-sensitive.

In my experience, that's almost always data entry, invoice processing, lead research, and structured content production.
